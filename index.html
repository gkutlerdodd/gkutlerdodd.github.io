Hi, I'm Griffin Kutler Dodd.

I'm a theoretical biology student pursing a Master's of Science in Bioinformatics & Biocomplexity at Utrecht University. I study the evolution of the immune system and host-virus interactions with bioinformatics and mathematical modeling.

Research

I investigate how the adaptive immune system evolves, focusing on MHC molecules, which are proteins that present viral fragments to immune cells. I analyze the diversity and function of these molecules in humans and other primates to study how they influence, and are shaped by, viral evolution.

Current Projects

  • Building a software tool to simulate viral evolution under selective pressure from MHC class I molecules using in silico epitope prediction.
  • Studying whether common HLA haplotypes have peptide-binding repertoires with special properties.

Publications

    In silico functional analysis of the human, chimpanzee, and gorilla MHC-A repertoires

    Griffin Kutler Dodd, Can Ke≈ümir

    Immunogenetics, 2025

    We used in silico epitope prediction methods to study the functional diversity of the MHC-A locus in primates. We found that both gorillas and chimpanzees surprisingly lack a peptide binding specificity (A02) that is common in human populations.

I use mathematical modeling to study how the immune system responds to viral infections. By focusing on HIV as a model, I explore how the virus and immune system interact, why some treatments succeed or fail, and how therapy might be improved.

Current Projects

  • Using mathematical modeling and machine learning to optimize HIV-1 broadly neutralizing antibodies to improve vaccine development.

Publications

    Immune Responses May Make HIV-1 Therapeutic Interfering Particles Less Effective

    Griffin Kutler Dodd, Rob de Boer

    Under review in Journal of Theoretical Biology

    We built a novel mathematical model of therapeutic interfering particles (TIPs), which are viral deletion mutants that compete with the wild-type virus to reduce viral load. We found that immune responses may limit the efficacy of TIPs for treating HIV-1 infection.

I study the interplay between the gut microbiome and the immune system, with a particular focus on how dysbiosis can lead to the development of (neuro)immune disease.

Current Projects

  • Testing for HLA associations with microbiome composition and immune markers in Alzheimer's disease and myalgic encephalomyelitis.

Publications

    Mucosal Immunity and the Gut-Microbiota-Brain-Axis in Neuroimmune Disease

    Kathryn G Sterling, Griffin Kutler Dodd, Shatha Alhamdi, Peter G Asimenios, Ruben K Dagda, Kenny L De Meirleir, Dorothy Hudig, Vincent C Lombardi

    International Journal of Molecular Sciences, 2022

    We reviewed the mechanisms by which mucosal immunity shape the gut microbiome and what is known about microbiome contributions to common neuroimmune diseases.

Recently, tools from theoretical computer science have been used to study biological evolution. I contribute to this by comparing evolution to a local search algorithm to study limitations on evolving "optimal" traits.

Current Projects

  • Studying how computational complexity affects the evolution of behavioral traits (costly learning and cooperation).
